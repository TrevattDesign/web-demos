About Jack

Depth, rigour, and genuine warmth

I am an integrative-relational psychotherapist registered with both the BACP and the UKCP, holding an MSc in psychotherapy alongside my undergraduate degree. I have worked across a wide range of settings including the NHS and private practice, with people from many different backgrounds and at many different stages of life.

My approach is genuinely integrative. I do not follow a single method but draw from a breadth of theoretical frameworks, including psychodynamic, humanistic, existential, relational, and systemic thinking, adapting to what each person actually needs rather than fitting people to a pre-set model.

What matters most to me is the quality of the therapeutic relationship. I believe that a calm, thoughtful and supportive space, where you feel truly heard and not judged, is the foundation from which real change becomes possible. I work with adults and older adults, and also with therapists in training.

I practise in person in Wimbledon SW19 and Fulham SW6, and online across the UK. Before we begin, I offer a free 15-minute introductory call so we can talk about what has brought you to therapy and whether working together feels like a good fit.

How I work

Truly integrative, genuinely relational

Integrative therapy means drawing from the best of multiple traditions rather than being bound by any one. I adapt my approach to what each person brings, working across four broad orientations.

Depth-oriented

Working with what lies beneath the surface: early experience, the unconscious, the existential, and the spiritual dimension of human life.

  • Psychodynamic
  • Existential
  • Phenomenological
  • Transpersonal

Relational

The therapeutic relationship itself as a space for change. Working with how we are with each other, and what that reveals about how we are with others.

  • Relational
  • Humanistic
  • Interpersonal
  • Emotionally Focused

Systems and parts

Understanding the person within their wider contexts, and the different internal parts that shape how we think, feel, and act.

  • Internal Family Systems
  • Systemic
  • Behavioural

Practical and time-aware

Therapy does not always need to be open-ended. I offer focused short-term and time-limited work where that is what is needed.

  • Brief therapy
  • Time-limited
  • Short-term
