About Toni

From hotel lobbies and bakery counters to the therapy room

Before becoming a counsellor, I spent several years managing a luxury boutique hotel, and then built and ran my own bakery and cafe. Both put me in front of people every day, asking the same kinds of questions: what do you need right now, and how can I help? Those years gave me something that no textbook could: a genuine ease with people, and a real interest in them.

I work in an integrative way, which means I don't take a one-size-fits-all approach. I draw from a range of evidence-based methods and adapt to what you actually need, rather than fitting you to a fixed model. I believe therapy works best when it respects the uniqueness of each person.

I work with adults of all ages, offering both individual and couples counselling. Sessions are available in person at an NHS GP surgery in Hendon NW4, as well as online and by phone. I am BACP registered and work in full accordance with their ethical framework, with regular supervision and ongoing professional development. I also offer sessions in French.

How I work

Integrative, and genuinely flexible

Integrative approach

I draw from a wide range of methods and adapt to what each person actually needs. This might include person-centred work, psychodynamic thinking, CBT-informed techniques, or others, depending on what's most useful at any given time.

Short-term or open-ended

Some people come with a specific focus and find that a handful of sessions is enough. Others benefit from longer, more exploratory work. We'll talk about what makes sense for you and revisit it as we go.

Aware of difference

I respect cultural background, individual preferences, and the many different ways people make sense of the world. The work is always led by you, and shaped around what's meaningful to you, not to a theory.

For individuals and couples

I work with individuals navigating personal difficulties, and with couples wanting to improve how they communicate, reconnect, or work through something difficult together.
