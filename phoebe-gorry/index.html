About Phoebe

Fifteen years on stage before the therapy room

Before training as a psychotherapist, I spent more than fifteen years working as a performer. That time gave me a particular understanding of the body, of presence, and of what it means to keep showing up in front of other people when things feel uncertain inside. It also gave me a direct experience of the specific pressures that come with creative and freelance work.

I hold a Master's in Counselling and Psychotherapy from the University of Roehampton and am a Registered Member of the BACP. I trained in somatic trauma therapy with world-renowned specialist Babette Rothschild, and that body-aware, trauma-informed approach underpins everything I do.

I work in private practice in Surbiton and London, and as a sessional psychotherapist at Nightingale Hospital. Previous experience includes NHS IAPT settings, the low-cost service Headstrong, and working with young creatives at the Royal Academy of Dramatic Arts.

How I work

Five approaches, one direction

I draw from several therapeutic traditions and adapt to what each individual actually needs. What unites them is attention to the whole person, not just the presenting problem.

01

Somatic & body-aware

Trauma and distress live in the nervous system as much as in memory. Trained by world-renowned somatic trauma specialist Babette Rothschild, I work with what the body holds alongside what words can reach. This is not physical or intrusive, just a more whole-person conversation.

02

Person-centred

You are the expert on your own experience. My role is to offer consistent warmth and non-judgmental presence, so that you can begin to hear yourself more clearly and trust what you find there. The relationship between therapist and client is itself part of the work.

03

Psychodynamic

Old patterns often have old roots. Where it's useful, we look at how early relationships and experiences continue to shape how you move through the world today. The aim is understanding, not blame, and the insight that comes from it is often where lasting change begins.

04

Creative & imaginal

Drawing on my background as a performer, I use imagery, metaphor, and embodied awareness to access what language alone sometimes can't reach. This is never obligatory, always led by what feels useful to you, and particularly effective for clients who think and feel in creative ways.

05

GSRD-affirmative

I offer a fully affirming space for LGBTQIA+ clients, those exploring gender and sexuality, and people whose identities or relationships fall outside mainstream frameworks. I bring knowledge, not just goodwill, and I understand the specific psychological pressures that minority stress and identity can carry.

Things people often want to know

Before any paid session, we have a free 30-minute video call to talk about what's brought you here and see whether working together feels right. If it does, the first full session is a chance to go a little deeper at whatever pace feels comfortable. There's no pressure to cover everything at once.

Conventional talk therapy works mainly through language and reflection. Somatic therapy also pays attention to what's happening in the body: breath, tension, sensation, posture. This matters because trauma and distress are stored in the nervous system, not only in memory. Working somatically lets us access things that words alone sometimes can't reach. It involves no physical contact, just a more body-aware conversation.

GSRD stands for Gender, Sexuality, and Relationship Diversities. Being GSRD-affirmative means I actively support LGBTQIA+ clients and people whose identities or relationships sit outside mainstream norms.
